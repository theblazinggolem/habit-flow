The Design Concept: The "Sliding Mask" Auth Card
The Goal: Create a single rectangular card that handles both Login and Registration. Instead of switching pages, a colored "branding panel" slides back and forth across the card like a physical shutter to reveal the form underneath.

Visual Layout for Auth Card:
- The Stage: One main box with a fixed size. Anything that moves "outside" of this box should be invisible.
- The Forms: Place the Login form and the Register form inside the box. They should be sitting side-by-side, but only one is fully visible at a time.
- The Shutter (Overlay): This is a colored panel (the "branding" side) that covers exactly half of the box. It contains text like "Welcome Back" or "Join Us."

The Interaction (The "Logic"):
- Sync with URL: The position of the shutter depends on the URL. If the user is at /login, the shutter is on the right. If they are at /register, the shutter is on the left.
- The Switch: When a user clicks the button to switch (e.g., from Login to Register):
    - The Shutter slides smoothly from right to left.
    - The Forms underneath also shift to make room for the shutter.
    - The Text inside the shutter should swap (e.g., "Welcome Back" disappears and "Hello Friend" appears).

The Movement Style:
- The movement should be "snappy but smooth" (not a jump-cut).
- While the shutter moves left, the content inside it should feel like it's moving slightly in the opposite direction to create a sense of depth (a parallax effect).
- The transition should take about half a second.
